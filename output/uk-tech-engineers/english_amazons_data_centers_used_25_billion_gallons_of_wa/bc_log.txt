The obvious joke is just "serverless uses water, ha." That's too soft. The board diagram is where the real punchline can live — not just the caption. If the board shows a "serverless architecture diagram" that ends with an arrow pointing to a tap (faucet) running into a drain labelled "water table," and the caption delivers the deadpan verdict, the joke fires on two levels simultaneously. The audience reads the board and recognises their own diagram before they've read the caption — and that recognition *is* the squirm. The caption should then land not as explanation but as verdict. Also: I should use the "hosepipe ban" contrast as the caption rather than the diagram, so both elements carry independent satirical weight.
