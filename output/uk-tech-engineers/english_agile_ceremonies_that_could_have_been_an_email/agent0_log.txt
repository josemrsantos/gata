<verdict>
CULTURAL ANGLE: Agile was meant to be the Protestant Reformation of software development — a righteous break from the bloated, Vatican-bureaucracy of waterfall methodology, liberating engineers to *actually ship things*. Instead, British developers find themselves attending a full liturgical calendar: the Daily Standup (a morning prayer nobody believes in), Sprint Planning (a sermon that overruns by ninety minutes), the Retrospective (confession, with action items nobody will complete), and the Refinement session (a reading from the Book of Tickets That Are Still Not Properly Defined). The word "ceremony" was right there in the manual and everyone politely ignored the implication. It is now 2024, the average British developer attends roughly six hours of Agile ceremony per fortnight, and the email that would have covered it remains unsent, sitting in a draft folder like a Jira ticket in the backlog — which is to say, forever.

REFERENCES:
- British office workers have a finely tuned tolerance for performative process — the same cultural muscle memory that produces "let's take this offline" and "just to circle back" also produces a workforce capable of sitting through a 47-minute standup without visibly weeping.
- The word "scrum" already belongs to rugby, a sport in which large men push against each other and nothing moves forward for a very long time — British engineers clocked this parallel immediately and said nothing, because saying nothing is also very British.
- The national reverence for the queue means "backlog grooming" lands differently here: it is simply the correct and natural order of things that work should wait in an orderly line, and that the line should grow.
- Channel 4's *Peep Show* gave the nation the definitive portrait of the open-plan office as a theatre of mutual performance and suppressed despair — every sprint review is basically Mark Corrigan internally monologuing while nodding at a burndown chart.
- The UK's long institutional relationship with the Church of England — a organisation famous for elaborate ritual, dwindling true believers, and a deep reluctance to formally wind the whole thing up — is structurally identical to most companies' relationship with Agile after year three.

JOKE TYPE: deadpan
