**REVIEW 1 — Too obvious / too lenient?**
My first instinct was to do a cartoon about rent prices or Airbnb. That's the obvious read. The sharper position is the one buried in the cultural context: the generation that did the 25 de Abril — who fought for the right to housing — legislated, tolerated, and voted for the exact conditions that stripped that right from their grandchildren. The joke isn't about Lisboa being expensive. The joke is about who built the cage and who's still singing the fado inside it. That's the squirm.

**REVIEW 3 — What material improvement still exists?**
The panels need a clear progressive arc with a genuine punchline twist — not just two observations. The twist should be the subversion: Panel 1 sets up what looks like a story about Lisboa being sold to foreigners. Panel 2 subverts it — the real culprit named isn't a tech nomad or a fund; it's the voter who approved every step of the way, reflected back at the audience. That's the squirm the brief demands. The caption in Panel 2 should land like a sentence in a courtroom, not a lament.
