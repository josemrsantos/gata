
Single-panel satirical cartoon, minimalist charcoal-on-chalkboard style, high-contrast, 1970s British newspaper newsroom setting. Selective Color style: greyscale background and environment, Gata rendered in full colour only.

GATA — domestic shorthair calico-tabby mix: white chest, muzzle, and paws; dark grey/black tabby stripes; orange/ginger patches on back. Small dark spot on the bridge of her pink nose. Simple dark leather collar with a gold/brass nameplate engraved "GATA". Demeanour: serious, investigative, slightly tired, highly intelligent. No human clothes, no hats, no glasses, no accessories of any kind.

Gata sits bolt-upright on a heavy metal newsroom desk facing a large dark chalkboard. One forepaw rests deliberately flat on the desk surface. Her expression is gimlet-eyed and exhausted — the look of a reporter who has followed this story for thirty years and is unsurprised by where it has ended.

THE CHALKBOARD — headed "ON THE SPOT" in large rough chalk capitals. Below: a hand-drawn chalk sketch of a triumphant figure at a lectern, fist raised, mouth open, circled emphatically. Chalk arrows radiate outward to labelled boxes, each attached to the board with strips of masking tape — no pins anywhere:
- "NHS WAITING LISTS — NOW YOURS"
- "INTEREST RATES — NOW YOURS"
- "POTHOLES — NOW YOURS"
- "THE SPREADSHEETS — ALL YOURS"
- "BRUSSELS — UNAVAILABLE"
- "THE ESTABLISHMENT — YOU ARE IT"
In the bottom-left corner of the chalkboard: a small chalk sketch of a three-wheeled van parked outside a large Georgian front door, with the words "DELIVERIES?" chalked above it in small uncertain lettering.

NEWSROOM BACKGROUND (greyscale): hunched figures at heavy typewriters, banks of fluorescent tube lighting overhead, stacked paper, overflowing ashtrays, a wall clock. Background is desaturated and recessive — all visual focus on Gata and the board.

DRY CAPTION in plain type at the bottom of the panel:
"After thirty years as the most gifted customer in the restaurant, Britain has apparently offered him the kitchen."

Style: single-panel editorial cartoon, dry British political satire, high-contrast lighting from overhead fluorescents, charcoal-sketch aesthetic on chalkboard elements, photorealistic Gata in full selective colour. No copyrighted characters or named artistic styles referenced.
