### PANEL DESCRIPTION

Gata sits bolt-upright on a heavy metal newsroom desk, facing the chalkboard with her characteristic expression of exhausted, gimlet-eyed intelligence. She is mid-investigation, one forepaw resting deliberately on the desk surface as though she has just placed the final piece of evidence.

The chalkboard behind her is headed **"ON THE SPOT"** in large chalk capitals. Below it, a roughly sketched figure — a triumphant man at a lectern, fist raised, mouth open mid-speech — is circled. Radiating out from the circle via chalk arrows are labelled boxes, each attached to the board with masking tape:

- **"NHS WAITING LISTS — NOW YOURS"**
- **"INTEREST RATES — NOW YOURS"**
- **"POTHOLES — NOW YOURS"**
- **"THE SPREADSHEETS — ALL YOURS"**
- **"BRUSSELS — UNAVAILABLE"**
- **"THE ESTABLISHMENT — YOU ARE IT"**

In the bottom-left corner of the board, a small sketch of a three-wheeled van parked outside a grand Georgian door, with a "DELIVERIES" sign above it. No label — the image speaks for itself.

The newsroom background is greyscale: hunched background figures at typewriters, fluorescent tube lighting, overflowing ashtrays, stacked paper.

Gata is in full selective colour against the grey.

**Caption (bottom of panel, dry and unhurried):**

> *"After thirty years as the most gifted customer in the restaurant, Britain has apparently offered him the kitchen."*
