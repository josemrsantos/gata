## SELF-REVIEW

**REVIEW 1:** The obvious angle is "chaos incoming / populist takeover" — that's been done to death. A sharper take leans into the *pattern*: Reform isn't a revolution, it's the latest in a long British tradition of protest parties that promise to burn the system down and then quietly become the system. The sharpest satirical move is to frame Reform's "takeover" not as seismic but as depressingly cyclical — a recycled product relabelled. The chalkboard can show this as a flow diagram of British protest parties all leading to the same dead end, with Reform simply being the newest arrow in the loop.

**REVIEW 2:** Gata must be *actively investigating*, not merely observing. She should be mid-gesture — a paw pointing at a specific, damning detail on the board — suggesting she's caught something the public hasn't noticed yet. The visual joke should land independently: the diagram shows a circular loop of protest parties (UKIP → Brexit Party → Reform UK) with each one labelled "REVOLUTION" on entry and "THE SYSTEM" on exit — and Reform's arrow is already curling back around. The caption confirms what the image implies.

**REVIEW 3:** Yes — this punches up at the political class and at Farage specifically, not at voters. It's accessible: anyone who remembers UKIP will get it immediately. The "revolution that becomes the establishment" joke is universal enough not to require specialist knowledge, and the circular diagram is visually self-explanatory.
