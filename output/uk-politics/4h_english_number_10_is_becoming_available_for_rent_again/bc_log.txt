<verdict>NEEDS REVISION</verdict>

**Rule Violated:** ORIGINALITY (Rule 4).

**Reasoning:** While the structural execution is excellent, the "revolving door of Prime Ministers" and the "NHS/Fiscal credibility" angle is the most common, first-read critique of UK politics. It is the "obvious" satire that every political cartoonist in the UK has already drawn. To meet the Gata standard, we must move past the *personnel* of the tenancy and target the *systemic architecture* of the property itself.

**Concrete Alternative:**
Instead of focusing on the *tenants* (the PMs) or the *deposit* (the NHS/Economy), focus on the **"Management Fee."** 

The cartoon should pivot to the idea that the "Georgian terraced house" (the State) is actually a shell company owned by a parent corporation that doesn't care who lives in the house, as long as the "Management Fee" (the tax/debt interest/privatization cycle) is paid. 

**Revised Punchline/Beat:**
In the final panel, Gata should reveal that the "Landlord" isn't a person or a party, but a **"Management Fee"** that increases every time the house is listed. The punchline should be that the "tenants" (the politicians) are actually just the *janitors* hired to clean up the mess so the next "Management Fee" can be collected. This shifts the blame from the politicians (who are just employees) to the structural, uncomfortable truth: the British public isn't being governed; they are being billed for the maintenance of a property they are no longer allowed to renovate.

[Satirist]
**REVIEW 1 — Too obvious / too lenient?**
The revision brief is sharp: shift from *personnel* (revolving PMs) to *structural architecture* (the management fee as the real constant). My first instinct on the revised brief would be to make the "management fee" a generic stand-in for "capitalism" or "the establishment" — which is itself a tired read. The sharper position: the management fee is *specific and itemised.* It has line items. Debt interest. Privatisation contracts. Consultancy spend. The joke isn't "the system is corrupt" (obvious) — it's that the fee schedule is *publicly available, has always been publicly available, and nobody in the listing ever mentions it.* The inconvenience is not that the fee exists. It is that we keep calling to enquire about the property without ever asking what the management fee is, because the agent has trained us to ask only about the tenant.
