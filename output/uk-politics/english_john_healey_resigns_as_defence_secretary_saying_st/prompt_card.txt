
Single-panel satirical cartoon. Minimalist charcoal-on-chalkboard style, high-contrast, dry caption at the bottom. Colour palette: fully greyscale background and environment; Gata rendered in full realistic colour only (Selective Color technique). Setting: 1970s newspaper newsroom — heavy metal desks, fluorescent strip lighting overhead, blurred background figures hunched over typewriters, ashtrays, stacked paper.

Foreground: Gata — a domestic shorthair calico-tabby mix with a white chest, white muzzle, and white paws; dark grey and black tabby stripes across her body; orange and ginger patches on her back and head; a small dark spot on the bridge of her pink nose; a simple dark leather collar with a gold/brass nameplate engraved "GATA" — sits on a heavy metal desk facing a large dark chalkboard. She wears no clothing, no accessories, no hat, no glasses, no pen — nothing. Her expression is flat, serious, and extremely tired. She stares directly at the board with the resigned composure of someone who knew this would happen in 2021.

The chalkboard, headed in large hand-drawn white chalk lettering: "ON THE SPOT"

Below the heading, centred on the board, is a single large, carefully and meticulously hand-drawn chalk Union Jack — detailed, accurate, slightly too large, taking up most of the board's surface. Beneath the Union Jack, in neat chalk block capitals, is a single label: "DEFENCE STRATEGY (2021–2026)". Nothing else is on the board. No diagrams. No bullet points. No spending figures. Just the flag and the label.

Taped to the lower-left corner of the chalkboard with a strip of masking tape is a small torn piece of paper on which is written in small chalk handwriting: "RECEIPT ENCLOSED — J. HEALEY".

Dry caption at the bottom of the panel, in plain sans-serif lettering:

"After three years of proving Labour had changed on defence, the Defence Secretary confirmed it hadn't."
