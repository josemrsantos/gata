**REVIEW 1 — Too obvious / too lenient?**
First instinct would be: Healey resigns, chalkboard shows a flat-pack diagram, Gata looks unimpressed. That's the surface. The sharper position is not about Healey at all — it's about *the buyer*. The public didn't get conned. They read the spec sheet, saw "no load-bearing components included," and said "fine." The uncomfortable truth isn't that the furniture is bad. It's that we chose it. The joke must land on the audience, not on Starmer or Healey. The final panel must make the viewer the subject of the satire.
